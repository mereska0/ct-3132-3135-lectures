$f$ — дифференцируема в точке $x_0 \implies \forall e \in \mathbb{R}^m \setminus \{0_m\} \; \exists \frac{\partial f}{\partial e}$ и $\forall i = 1,\ldots,m \; \exists \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i}$

## Доказательство

$f(x_0 + \Delta x) = f(x_0) + A_f \Delta x + o(|\Delta x|), \quad \Delta x \to 0_m$

Возьмём $e \in \mathbb{R}^m \setminus \{0\}$, $e^0 = \frac{e}{|e|}$, $\Delta x = t e^0$, $t \in \mathbb{R}$.

$$
f(x_0 + t e^0) - f(x_0) = A_f (t e^0) + o(|t e^0|), \quad t \to 0
$$

$$
f(x_0 + t e^0) - f(x_0) = t A_f e^0 + o(|t|)
$$

$$
\frac{\partial f}{\partial e} = \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{f(x_0 + t e^0) - f(x_0)}{t} = \lim_{t \to 0} \left( A_f e^0 + \frac{o(|t|)}{t} \right) = A_f e^0
$$

Следовательно,

$$
\frac{\partial f}{\partial e_i} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i} = A_f e_i
$$
